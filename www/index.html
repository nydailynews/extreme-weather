The first winter storm of 2018 is bringing extreme cold, high winds and snow in a 'bomb cyclone'

  • facebook
  • Tweet
  • email

By KELLI R. PARKER

The bombogenesis is at hand and the end is... probably not nigh.

What's that? You have no idea what that means? Well, you're not alone. Social media is all worked up and #BombCyclone has been trending on Twitter. But what is that?

Bombogenesis — a fancy term for a winter hurricane — can happen when a cold air mass collides with a warm air mass, like air over warm ocean water. The formation of the rapidly strengthening weather system is what creates a bomb cyclone.

Got all that? Great. But what does all this mean for us? Winter Storm Grayson is really giving us the business. Gov. Cuomo declared a state of emergency for New York City and its suburbs.

PHOTOS: The bomb cyclone snow day in New York City

Schools were closed and Tuesday's morning commute resembled a scene from the movie "The Day After Tomorrow." The winter storm warning issued by the National Weather Service is in effect until Friday morning. And MTA trains and buses are operating under a Cold Weather Plan.

Central Park set a new snow record for the day with 9.8 inches of snow, according to the National Weather Service, and Long Island could see more than a foot of the white stuff before the storm is done. Temperatures are expected to be well below normal for the weekend with wind chills between zero and -5 degrees. Yikes! It should warm up by Monday, though.
