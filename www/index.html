The first winter storm of 2018 is bringing extreme cold, high winds and snow in a 'bomb cyclone'

  • facebook
  • Tweet
  • email

By KELLI R. PARKER

The bombogenesis is at hand and the end is ... probably not nigh.

What's that? You have no idea what that means? Well, you're not alone. Social media is all worked up and #BombCyclone has been trending on Twitter for the past 24 hours. But what is that?

Bombogenesis — a fancy term for a winter hurricane — can happen when a cold air mass collides with a warm air mass, like air over warm ocean water. The formation of the rapidly strengthening weather system is what creates a bomb cyclone.

Got all that? Great. But what does all this mean for us? The National Weather Service has issued a winter storm watch and is predicting heavy, blowing snow on Thursday with wind gusts as high as 45 miles per hour. Temperatures are expected to be 20 to 40 degrees below normal.
